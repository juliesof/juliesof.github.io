How I used to fill my days

Unless someone like you cares a whole awful lot, Nothing is going to get better. It's not.

In 2009 I spent a year working for ACCION USA through AmeriCorps. I processed loan applications for small business owners, in English and Spanish. I also started their social media program.

Previously, I was an educator and public speaker about personal finance. I gave talks on creating a livable budget, understanding credit reports, and preventing identity theft.
The YouTube video, which aired on the Medfield public access station, shows me giving a class to senior citizens on preventing identity theft. They were a sharp and fun bunch of folks!

There are many reasons why I want to become a web developer. Of course having a job in a field that's growing every year is a big plus. And having a job that is intellectually stimulating is also very important to me. But one of the most important things to me is being able to use the skills that I have to make the world a better place. Starting with General Assembly and then on through the years I plan on becoming an expert in multiple programming languages. Languages that exist now and those that will come. And I will be able to leverage those skills to make change where the world needs it.
